# 🦀 Programming Rust (2nd Edition) — Learning Schedule

**Book:** Programming Rust, 3rd Edition — Jim Blandy, Jason Orendorff, Leonora Tindall

**Duration:** ~8 weeks

**Session length:** 30 minutes

Each session includes:
- 📖 Reading (~15–20 min)
- 💻 Coding practice (~10–15 min)

---

## Foundations

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 1 | Preface & Ch. 1 – Why Rust? | Understand Rust’s goals, safety guarantees, and design motivations. |
| 2 | Ch. 2 – A Tour of Rust (Part 1) | Learn Rust’s basic syntax and run your first programs. |
| 3 | Ch. 2 – A Tour of Rust (Part 2) | Practice variables, functions, and control flow. |
| 4 | Ch. 3 – Expressions and Statements | Explore Rust’s expression-oriented design. |
| 5 | Review & Mini Project | Build a small CLI app (e.g., temperature converter). |

---

## Ownership and Borrowing

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 6 | Ch. 4 – Ownership (Part 1) | Learn ownership and move semantics. |
| 7 | Ch. 4 – Borrowing & References | Master borrowing rules and immutability. |
| 8 | Ch. 4 – Lifetimes (Intro) | Understand lifetimes and reference validity. |
| 9 | Ch. 5 – Structs | Create structs, methods, and associated functions. |
| 10 | Review & Coding | Build a struct-based app (e.g., book catalog). |

---

## Enums, Patterns, and Error Handling

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 11 | Ch. 6 – Enums & Pattern Matching (Part 1) | Learn `enum`, `match`, and destructuring. |
| 12 | Ch. 6 – Enums & Pattern Matching (Part 2) | Practice nested pattern matching with `Option` and `Result`. |
| 13 | Ch. 7 – Error Handling (Part 1) | Use `Result` and `?` for safe error handling. |
| 14 | Ch. 7 – Error Handling (Part 2) | Implement custom error types. |
| 15 | Project | Build a CLI tool that reports errors gracefully. |

---

## Collections, Strings, and Iterators

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 16 | Ch. 8 – Collections | Explore `Vec`, `HashMap`, and slices. |
| 17 | Ch. 9 – Strings and Text | Work with `String`, `&str`, and UTF-8. |
| 18 | Ch. 10 – Iterators (Part 1) | Learn iterator adapters and methods. |
| 19 | Ch. 10 – Iterators (Part 2) | Implement your own iterator. |
| 20 | Review & Project | Build a word-frequency counter or CSV parser. |

---

## Traits and Generics

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 21 | Ch. 11 – Traits (Part 1) | Define and implement traits. |
| 22 | Ch. 11 – Traits (Part 2) | Explore trait bounds and object safety. |
| 23 | Ch. 12 – Generics | Use generic types and constraints effectively. |
| 24 | Ch. 13 – Operator Overloading & Closures | Learn closures and captured variables. |
| 25 | Review & Project | Build a generic data-processing library. |

---

## Memory, Concurrency, and Smart Pointers

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 26 | Ch. 14 – Smart Pointers (Part 1) | Learn `Box`, `Rc`, and `Arc`. |
| 27 | Ch. 14 – Smart Pointers (Part 2) | Explore `RefCell` and interior mutability. |
| 28 | Ch. 15 – Concurrency (Part 1) | Use threads and message passing. |
| 29 | Ch. 15 – Concurrency (Part 2) | Share state safely with `Mutex` and `Arc`. |
| 30 | Project | Build a multithreaded word counter. |

---

## Unsafe Rust, FFI, and Modules

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 31 | Ch. 16 – Unsafe Code | Understand when and why unsafe is needed. |
| 32 | Ch. 17 – FFI (Foreign Function Interface) | Call C functions from Rust. |
| 33 | Ch. 18 – Crates and Modules | Organise and structure larger Rust projects. |
| 34 | Ch. 19 – Testing & Documentation | Write tests, benchmarks, and documentation. |
| 35 | Review & Project | Build and test a reusable Rust library. |

---

## Performance, Async, and Macros

| Session | Topic | Goal |
|----------|--------|------|
| 36 | Ch. 20 – Profiling and Optimisation | Learn to measure and improve performance. |
| 37 | Ch. 21 – Asynchronous Programming | Explore async/await and futures. |
| 38 | Ch. 22 – Macros (Declarative) | Write simple macros with `macro_rules!`. |
| 39 | Ch. 23 – Procedural Macros | Build a derive macro. |
| 40 | Final Project | Combine everything into a small async app (e.g., web client). |

---

## 🧠 Tips for Success
- Keep a local Cargo project open for quick testing.
- After every 5 sessions, review and refactor your code.
- Use [The Rust Book](https://doc.rust-lang.org/book/) alongside for extra examples.
- Join [Rust Discord](https://discord.gg/rust-lang) or [r/learnrust](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnrust/) for help.
- Don’t rush — consistency beats speed!
